Among many skills that are crucial for research success, I describe a few essential ones below.

# Integrative Thinking

Problem-solving skill is well-known as a critical component of successful research. However, *integrative thinking*, which involves connecting fragmented ideas and concepts and synthesising them into a coherent framework, often gets overlooked. This skill is essential for developing innovative solutions, especially in the field of software engineering (like any other engineering discipline) where complex problems often require multifaceted approaches and balancing trade-offs.

[Martin (2007)](https://rogerlmartin.com/thought-pillars/integrative-thinking) highlights the importance of integrative thinking as follows:
> I coined the term Integrative Thinking to describe the form of reasoning I observed in my multiyear study of highly successful leaders.  I found a strikingly consistent pattern to their thinking approach when they faced unpleasant either/or choices. When faced with opposing options or models, most people simply choose, even if they don’t particularly love either model. 
In contrast, these Integrative Thinkers, as I came to call them, aren’t satisfied with simply making an unpleasant trade-off.  Instead, they master the ability to constructively face the tensions of opposing models, and instead of choosing one at the expense of the other, they generate a creative solution of the tensions in the form of a new model that contains elements of the individual models, but is superior to each.

Often, integrative thinking is essential for navigating existing solutions/approaches and identifying opportunities for improvement or innovation. It can lead to the development of novel frameworks, methodologies, or technologies that better address the complexities of real-world problems. Examples of such a framework include:

> Shin, Donghwan, Zanis Ali Khan, Domenico Bianculli, and Lionel Briand. "A theoretical framework for understanding the relationship between log parsing and anomaly detection." In International conference on runtime verification, pp. 277-287. Cham: Springer International Publishing, 2021.

> Shin, Donghwan, and Doo-Hwan Bae. "A theoretical framework for understanding mutation-based testing methods." In 2016 IEEE International Conference on Software Testing, Verification and Validation (ICST), pp. 299-308. IEEE, 2016.

> Staats, Matt, Michael W. Whalen, and Mats PE Heimdahl. "Programs, tests, and oracles: the foundations of testing revisited." In Proceedings of the 33rd international conference on software engineering, pp. 391-400. 2011.

Such frameworks can be a valuable publication per se, but they also serve as a foundation for future research directions (e.g. developing a novel approach by building upon the insights gained from these frameworks).

# Resilience

Resilience is the ability to adapt and bounce back from setbacks, failures, or challenges. In the context of research, resilience is crucial for navigating the inevitable obstacles and difficulties that arise during the research process. This includes dealing with failed experiments, rejected papers, and unexpected results.

Developing resilience involves cultivating a growth mindset, which is the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication and hard work. This mindset fosters a love for learning and a willingness to embrace challenges, ultimately leading to greater success in research.

Strategies for building resilience include:

1. **Embracing Failure**: Viewing failures as opportunities for learning and growth rather than as setbacks.
2. **Seeking Support**: Building a strong support network of peers, mentors, and advisors who can provide guidance and encouragement.
3. **Practicing Self-Care**: Prioritizing physical and mental well-being through regular exercise, healthy eating, and mindfulness practices.
4. **Setting Realistic Goals**: Breaking down larger research goals into smaller, manageable tasks to maintain motivation and a sense of accomplishment.

By fostering resilience, researchers can better navigate the challenges of their work and ultimately achieve their goals.
